Slide 01 · Known Risks

Bias & Hallucination

Bias

Models learn patterns from human-generated data — including human prejudices.

  • Gender stereotypes in job descriptions
  • Under-representation of minorities
  • Geographic & cultural bias (Western-centric data)
  • Recency bias from training window
Hallucination

Models generate plausible-sounding but false content with full confidence.

  • Invented citations and papers
  • Wrong legal / medical / technical facts
  • Confident errors in code logic
  • Mitigated by: RAG, grounding, verification
Rule: Never use AI output without human review in high-stakes decisions (hiring, medicine, law, finance).

Slide 02 · Governance

EU AI Act &
Governance

EU AI Act — In force Aug 2024

The world's first comprehensive AI law. Risk-based approach with four tiers.

🔴 Unacceptable — Prohibited. Social scoring, subliminal manipulation.
🟠 High Risk — Heavy regulation. Healthcare, employment, education, law enforcement.
🟡 Limited Risk — Transparency required. Chatbots must disclose they are AI.
🟢 Minimal Risk — Free to use. Games, spam filters, AI-generated content.
Global Landscape
  • 🇺🇸 USA — Executive Order on AI Safety (Oct 2023). Voluntary commitments.
  • 🇨🇳 China — Generative AI Regulations since 2023.
  • 🇬🇧 UK — Principles-based approach. Sector-specific regulators.

Slide 03 · Case Studies

Real-World
AI Failures

Amazon Hiring Tool (2018)

CV screening model penalised female candidates. Trained on 10 years of male-dominated hiring data — learned gender was a negative signal.

→ Reuters (Oct 2018)
COMPAS Recidivism (2016)

Criminal risk scoring system was twice as likely to falsely flag Black defendants as high-risk compared to white defendants.

→ ProPublica — Machine Bias (2016)
Lawyer Cites Fake Cases (2023)

A New York attorney used ChatGPT for legal research. The AI invented six fake case citations — complete with made-up judges and rulings. Sanctions followed.

→ NYT — Mata v. Avianca (May 2023)
Deepfake Fraud ($25M, 2024)

Hong Kong firm employee tricked by AI-generated video call impersonating the CFO. Result: $25 million wire transfer to scammers.

→ CNN — HK Deepfake Fraud (Feb 2024)
Lesson: Every failure above was preventable with proper governance: bias audits, human oversight, output verification, and authentication protocols.

Slide 03b · 2024 Turning Points

AI Ethics in 2024:
Three Landmark Cases

🗳️
Deepfake Robocall · New Hampshire Primary, Jan 2024

A voice clone of President Biden told registered Democrats not to vote in the primary. ~25,000 robocalls sent using voice-cloning technology. The FCC subsequently banned AI-generated voice robocalls.

⚖ Precedent: Regulatory response to AI-generated political disinformation.
📰
NYT vs OpenAI · Copyright Lawsuit, Dec 2023 → ongoing

The New York Times sued OpenAI and Microsoft for training on millions of copyrighted articles. GPT-4 was shown to reproduce near-verbatim NYT content. Core question: is training on copyrighted data fair use?

⚖ Precedent: Could reshape how AI systems can legally use publicly available content for training.
✈️
Air Canada Chatbot Ruling · Feb 2024

A Canadian tribunal ruled Air Canada liable for its AI chatbot's misinformation about bereavement fares. Air Canada's "separate legal entity" defence was rejected — companies own their AI's outputs.

⚖ Precedent: Companies are legally liable for what their AI systems tell users.

Slide 04 · Frameworks

AI Governance
Frameworks

NIST AI RMF 1.0 (2023)
  • Four functions: Govern · Map · Measure · Manage
  • De facto US standard — mirrors NIST Cybersecurity Framework
ISO/IEC 42001 (2023)
  • First certifiable AI management standard — aligns with ISO 27001
  • Covers full AI lifecycle: design → deploy → retire
EU AI Act (in force Aug 2024)
  • Risk tiers: Unacceptable · High · Limited · Minimal
  • Banned: social scoring, real-time biometric surveillance
  • Fines: up to €35M or 7% global annual turnover
→ Official Text (EUR-Lex)

Slide 05 · Responsible AI

Responsible AI
in Practice

Transparency
  • Disclose when AI is in use
  • Explain model decisions (XAI)
  • Document training data sources
Fairness
  • Audit for demographic bias
  • Diverse training data and teams
  • Measure disparate impact
Safety & Control
  • Human-in-the-loop for high stakes
  • Kill switch & rollback plan
  • Red-team before deployment
Principle: Responsible AI is not a compliance checkbox — it is a product quality standard.

Wrapping Up · Six Chapters

Key Takeaways

Ch. 1 · How LLMs Work

LLMs are probabilistic token predictors. They don't "know" facts — they encode statistical patterns. Scale is what unlocked capabilities.

Ch. 2 · Prompt Engineering

How you ask matters as much as what you ask. Role, context, format, and examples dramatically improve output quality.

Ch. 3 · AI-Assisted Coding

AI coding tools excel at boilerplate and tests. They're unreliable for novel algorithms and security-critical code. Always review AI-generated output.

Ch. 4 · RAG Architecture

RAG connects LLMs to private data via vector search, solving the knowledge cutoff problem. Retrieval quality determines answer quality.

Ch. 5 · AI Agents & MCP

Agents combine LLMs with tools and planning for multi-step autonomy. MCP standardises tool connections. Human oversight is essential.

Ch. 6 · Ethics & Future

AI amplifies biases and creates new risks. Responsible AI requires transparency, fairness audits, and human oversight. The technology is neutral — the choices are ours.

Above all: AI is a powerful tool, not magic. Understanding its mechanics, limitations, and risks makes you a better builder, user, and decision-maker.
